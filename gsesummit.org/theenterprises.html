Aqualus is a social enterprise that aims to solve the challenge of eutrophication by creating floating islands that contain canna plants which can absorb excessive amounts of nitrogen and phosphorus nutrients which are common in eutrophication. This was created by Esha and Ishaan at GSES 2018.

Jaldhan is a social enterprise that aims to reduce the sewage that is discharged into water bodies by directing sewage water to treatment plants spread across the city. This was created by Kushal and Nithin at GSES 2018.





Teach 2 is a mobile application that aims to help teachers improve their English language skills so that they can pass on information effectively, creating a better workforce.



Aqualimpia is a social enterprise that aims to spread awareness on how disposal of garbage in the lakes by slum dwellers living nearby can lead to multiple health problems. This was created by Vedika, Mahee and Manan at GSES 2018.



Svasthy, Svachhtha, Shiksha is a social enterprise that aims to increase the hygiene level in schools around India through offering workshops and providing medical kits. This was created by Ahaan, Ahana and Sandhya at GSES 2018.



Pragmatic is a social enterprise that aims to create online courses for teachers to improve their content knowledge and teaching expertise. This was create by Udhava and Aryan at GSES 2018.



Citta Verde is a social enterprise that aims to make use of the land that has been exploited by huge heaps of garbage through the process of bio-mining. This was created by Abhishek, Tej and Rivka at GSES 2018.



Trustop is a social enterprise that aims to provide intermediary services to help private companies working in the education space work more effectively with the government and government run public schools. This was created by Sai Rishika at GSES 2018.



Purnavriti is a social enterprise that aims to incentivize people living in residential colonies to segregate their waste by paying people for their wet waste, and then selling the compost and biogas obtained.This was created by Anirudh, Vaishali and Sahil at GSES 2018.



Bike It! is a social enterprise that aims to create a bicycle sharing system which will provide a healthy and efficient alternative to buses and auto rickshaws. This was created by Anirudh and Silas at GSES 2018.





Zero Fame is a mobile application that aims to partner with retail outlets to monitor and reuse food that is about to expire and is still edible in an efficient manner. This was created by Mokshi and Manvi at GSES 2018.



Gaytri's Kitchen is a restaurant that will serve delicious and innovative vegetarian cuisine and spread awareness on the negative aspects of meat consumption. This was created by Angela, Tanisha and Aayush at GSES 2018.



DROP is a social enterprise that aims to provide low income farmers with products to purify water in order to make it more sanitary for agricultural use – increasing crop yields and well being of the farmer. DROP has created a water purification system using the following accessible materials: Rocks, Pebbles/Gravel, Sand, Cotton, Charcoal. This was created by students at GSES 2016.



OriGen is an organization that provides professional development services (workshops and seminars) to teachers based on feedback collected from students through surveys and interactive sessions with students. This was created by Aakash, Abhishek, Aryaman, Kabir who are Grade 11 students at GSES 2017.





Resonance is a product that cleans water coming from the river to helps farmer who suffer because of unavailability of clean water. This was created by Satwik, Harshee, Akshat, Samridhi who are Grade 11 students at GSES 2017.



Nivaran is an application that aims to connect doctors, governments and citizens to information that could save lives by preventing malaria and dengue in India. This was created by students at GSES 2016.



Local Farmers Associations is a social enterprise that aims to provide smallholder farmers with adequate amounts of information such as improved farming techniques, how to use farming equipment, benefits of being involved directly with the market through workshops that will be run with NGO's and government bodies. This was created by Kenz, Faizh, Dhwani, Nishtha who are Grade 11 students at GSES 2017.



Pakhi is a social enterprise that aims to empower young girls from rural areas by:
- Creating connections between young female students and older girls
- Connecting students to videos on soft skills and confidence building
- Developing a relationship with a girls’ school in Dhaka, hopefully Jaago school aided by Thrive foundation. This was created by students at GSES 2016.





Body Bodha is a social enterprise that aims to raise societal awareness on proper sanitation for women. This was created by students at GSES 2016.




Fair Acre is a social enterprise that helps farmers by helping them work in their comfort zones and helping them be self sustainable by providing vocational education for them. This was created by students at GSES 2016.



Wingman is a social enterprise that aims to connect corporations, nonprofits and volunteers to ensure efficient flow of CSR funds and community support. This was created by Roshan at GSES 2015.





Fast Forward is a mobile application that provides an incentive for users to use more sustainable modes of transport by providing them credit/points that can be redeemed at particular business outlets. This was created by Ishita, Bhavya, Siddharth who are Grade 11 Students at GSES 2017.
