Description

TRAI for Science and Engineering will be a two day event with a focus on giving students hands-on experience working with technologies that will be a large part of their future. Students will also begin to think about how they can use these technologies to make a positive impact on the world around them.

Science and engineering students will work with technology mentors and Design Thinking coaches. The coaches bring a diverse set of backgrounds and skills ranging from engineering design and architecture, to programming, software development and robotics.

Students will be introduced to two branches of technology.

  • Robotics and Automation
  • The Internet of Things

Design Thinking

According to the Henry Ford Learning Institute, Design Thinking is an approach to design that uses specific techniques and mindsets to develop creative solutions. Identifying problems and generating solutions for design challenges allows students to frame their thinking around developing empathy, promoting a bias toward action, encouraging ideation, and working in a culture of prototyping. We believe technology driven solutions aren't the work of lone geniuses. They are the result of skilled collaborative teams systematically working to understand human needs and building on each other's knowledge, thinking and skills to design solutions. The design thinking process is key for guiding and supporting this collaboration and innovation.
